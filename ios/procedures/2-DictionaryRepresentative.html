Inheritance and Reference

A model's DR should inherit the model's superclass's DR. so that its superclass's DR will process the properties defined in superclass; and this model's DR will process its own properties - no need to process its superclass' properties in duplciation way.

A model's DR should has-a the DRs of model's relationships. When coming to process the relationship, the relationship's DR will be used, rather than processing the relationship in this model's DR itself.

For example

       
B is-a A DR_B is-a DR_A    

 

B has-a X

 DR_B has-a DR_X

Big Picture

While I am working on "property (relationship) incarnation" in Ruby for ExcelExport project, I realized that the property mapping is such a common place.. The mapping is worthy to be defined in an object !!

You know, such property mapping work DOES create new objects, but not new data or semantics. It is just a pure engineering work. Some works like an adaptor, connecting between different systems. Other passes data from one role (Model) in arch to another role (Dictionary/Hash)
